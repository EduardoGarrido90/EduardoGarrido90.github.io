Welcome to my web page my dear outsider! I am Eduardo C. Garrido Merchán, a researcher currently working as a Teacher Assistant @ UAM and studying a Machine Learning PhD (mainly focused in Bayesian Optimization) at the Machine Learning group.

About me: My hobbies are spending time with my family, science fiction (mostly books), artificial intelligence and philosophy. My research activities include Bayesian optimization, Gaussian processes, probabilistic machine learning, causality and other minor lines of research. I am also informed about natural language processing and machine learning in general, as I am a very curious person.

I am also the host of the Probabilistic Machine Learning course of Philip Hennig paper club. Each week we revise the PML concepts covered in the course of Philip. Have also been the organizer of the Machine Learning research seminars at the machine learning research group at UAM.
My main research interests and activities are the following ones:
    Bayesian Optimization: This is my main area of research. With Bayesian Optimization we can auto-tune any machine learning algorithm. Furthermore, we can even perform automatic machine learning or optimize real industrial applications. Have you ever dream on pressing a red button and getting the perfect configuration for an algorithm or process without even thinking about how it works? Here is your solution. My work resides in expansions of the Bayesian Optimization framework. Main papers:
  • Garrido-Merchán, Eduardo C., and Daniel Hernández-Lobato. Dealing with categorical and integer-valued variables in Bayesian optimization with Gaussian processes. Neurocomputing 2019.
  • Garrido-Merchán, E.C. and Hernández-Lobato, D., Predictive entropy search for multi-objective bayesian optimization with constraints. Neurocomputing. 2019

    Causality: Correlations are not the only important thing in machine learning. I can push a train when it starts moving and retain it when it stops. My movements are correlated with those of the train but... the train stops moving because of my actions? Although there is correlation there is not causation. Causation is important. I study different ways to represent causation using graphs. Main papers:
  • Garrido-Merchán, Eduardo C. et al. "Uncertainty Weighted Causal Graphs". arXiv preprint (2020)
  • Puente, Cristina, et al. "Summarizing information by means of causal sentences through causal graphs." Journal of Applied Logic 24 (2017): 3-14.
  • Puente, Cristina, et al. "Creating a natural language summary from a compressed causal graph." 2013 joint ifsa world congress and nafips annual meeting (ifsa/nafips). IEEE, 2013.
    Gaussian Processes: Bayesian Optimization uses Probabilistic Models in order to work. Gaussian Processes are an example. There are lots of stochastic processes that serve as priors over functions. These are like probability distributions that serve as priors over data, but for functions. These are very useful and beautiful to study models. My work resides on expanding Gaussian Processes to different scenarios. Main papers:
  • Garrido-Merchán, Eduardo C., and Daniel Hernández-Lobato. "Dealing with categorical and integer-valued variables in bayesian optimization with gaussian processes." Neurocomputing 380 (2020): 20-35.
  • Villacampa-Calvo, Carlos, et al. "Multi-class Gaussian Process Classification with Noisy Inputs." arXiv preprint arXiv:2001.10523 (2020).
    Machine Consciousness: This is personal work but I consider to know about this quite a lot. Consciousness is a topic that lies in the borderline of the scientific method, but now even Yoshua Bengio studies it. It is an interdisciplinary area targeted as well by brain inspired AI. Philosophy of Mind has worked in this topic for quite a long time. Neuroscientist are also working a lot here.
  • Merchán, Eduardo C. Garrido, and Martín Molina. "A Machine Consciousness architecture based on Deep Learning and Gaussian Processes." arXiv preprint arXiv:2002.00509 (2020).
  • Advised this Msc. Thesis: Francisco Mendoza, Martín Molina. Artificial Consciousness Msc. Thesis. UPM. 2020.
